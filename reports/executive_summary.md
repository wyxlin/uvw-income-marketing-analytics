# Executive Summary

## Objective

Evaluate how demographic and employment characteristics relate to annual income, and translate those findings into audience segments that UVW College can use for targeted enrollment campaigns.

## Main Findings

### 1. Education is one of the strongest signals
Individuals with high-school education or below are much more concentrated in the `<=50K` income group, while bachelor's, master's, professional, and doctorate categories have much stronger representation in the `>50K` group.

### 2. Mid-career adults are important prospects
Income tends to improve through early and mid-career years, which makes working adults a meaningful audience for upskilling, degree completion, and employer-aligned programs.

### 3. Occupation matters
Professional, executive, and specialized occupations show stronger association with higher income, while service and clerical roles are more concentrated in lower-income brackets.

### 4. A segment-based marketing strategy is better than a one-size-fits-all message
Different groups require different positioning:

- **Early-career / lower-income learners**: affordability, online access, job mobility
- **Working adults with some college**: degree completion, flexible scheduling, ROI messaging
- **Career-transition audiences**: certificates, applied programs, fast upskilling

## Recommended Marketing Segments

### Segment A: Working adults earning <=50K with high-school or some-college backgrounds
Messaging:
- career advancement
- tuition flexibility
- online or evening programs
- short-term credentials that ladder into degrees

### Segment B: Adults with some college but no bachelor's degree
Messaging:
- transfer-friendly pathways
- degree completion
- credit for prior learning
- salary growth potential

### Segment C: Occupations with lower pay concentration but stable employment
Messaging:
- role-based advancement
- practical programs tied to business, IT support, healthcare administration, and operations

## Next Step

Convert this analysis into a small dashboard or lead-scoring prototype that allows marketing teams to explore segment characteristics and tailor outreach campaigns.
